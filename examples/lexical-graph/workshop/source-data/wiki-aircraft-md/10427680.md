The Air Tractor AT-300 is a family of agricultural aircraft that first flew in the United States in September 1973. Type certification was awarded to Air Tractor in November the same year, and serial production commenced in 1976. Of low-wing monoplane taildragger configuration, they carry a chemical hopper between the engine firewall and the cockpit.


## Variants
AT-300 - prototype and initial production models wit 450 hp (340 kW) Pratt & Whitney R-985 engine and 320 US gal (270 imp gal; 1,200 L) hopper.
AT-301 - powered by 600 kW (800 hp) Pratt & Whitney R-1340 engine.
AT-301B - AT-301 with 350 US gal (290 imp gal; 1,300 L) hopper.
AT-302 - turboprop version with 600 shp (450 kW) Lycoming LTP101 engine.
AT-302A - AT-302 with 385 US gal (321 imp gal; 1,460 L) hopper.
